Verifying your identity for Companies House

Telling people how to verify their identity

You can use the Verify your identity for Companies House service to:

  • verify online
  • find out how to verify in person at a Post Office

Another option is to ask a Companies House authorised agent (such as an accountant or solicitor) to verify your identity for you. This is also known as an Authorised Corporate Service Provider (ACSP).

Why people need to verify their identity

You need to verify your identity to confirm you are who you say you are. This will:

  • reduce the risk of fraud
  • improve transparency, trust and accuracy of information on the Companies House register

Who needs to verify their identity

These are the roles that will need to verify their identity once IDV has been fully implemented.

You need to verify your identity if you are:

  • a person with significant control (PSC)
  • a director
  • the equivalent of a director – this includes members, general partners and managing officers
  • someone who files for a company
  • applying to register as a Companies House authorised agent (also known as an Authorised Corporate Service Provider, or ACSP)
